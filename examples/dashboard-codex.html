1. Price Elasticity of Demand

The specification is ln(volume) = a + b ln(price) + geography FE + type FE + year FE. In a log-log model, the price coefficient is elasticity: a 1% price change maps to a b% change in volume.

Naive model

-4.59

95% CI -4.66 to -4.52 | R² 0.324

Without controls, a 10% price increase is associated with 35.4% lower weekly volume.

Controlled model

-1.04

95% CI -1.06 to -1.02 | R² 0.972

After holding geography, organic vs conventional mix, and calendar year constant, a 10% price increase maps to roughly 9.4% lower demand. That is close to unit elasticity.

Interpretation

Controls matter because expensive observations are not random. Organic avocados are priced about 45.3% above conventional but account for only 3.3% of total volume, and higher-cost markets also have structurally smaller baseline demand. The uncontrolled coefficient loads those differences onto price.

Robustness check: restricting the model to local markets and removing broad regional rollups still yields an elasticity of -1.05.

3. Where and When Avocados Move

The biggest local markets are concentrated in large Sun Belt metros, while prices are highest in coastal and Northeast markets. Demand also follows a seasonal rhythm: strongest in late winter through spring, with prices peaking in late summer.
